2. Data for Quantitative Compasion

In this section, we provide detailed information of the datasets we used in the quantitative comparison in Section 4.1, including the data source and corresponding pre-processing method.



There are mainly two ways for data pre-processing. One is for ordinary text data. We extracted the content of the text in each web page, segmented words according to spaces, removed punctuation marks and stop words. Then we counted the word frequency of words, ignoring those with extremely low frequencies. The ﬁnal weight of the word is deﬁned by its frequency. Brexit, Harry Potter, Apple Inc., Friends(TV Series), and Michael Jackson are processed in this way. The second way of processing is especially for rank data like NBA players. We map the rank of a word to its weight. For example for NBA players, we use the following function to deﬁne the weight \(w_i\) of the person who ranked \(r_i\) :\[w_i = w_{min} +(r_{max} −r _i )/(r_{max} −r_{min} )(w_{max} −w_{min} )\]

where \(w_{min}\) is the minimal non-zero weight (e.g.,0.3), \(w_{ max}\) is the maximal weight. These two parameters can be set. \(r_{max}\) is the rank of the last place. \( r_{min}\) is the rank of the ﬁrst place.
